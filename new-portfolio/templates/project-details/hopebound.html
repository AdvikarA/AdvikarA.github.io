Hopebound Data Visualization Platform

As part of Harvard's Tech for Social Good initiative, I led a team developing an interactive data visualization platform for Hopebound, a non-profit organization addressing the youth mental health crisis.

Role: Project Manager, leading a team of 6 software engineers
Data Visualization Dashboard
Impact Metrics Visualization
Admin Management Interface

Project Overview

The youth mental health crisis represents one of the most pressing challenges facing our society today. Hopebound provides free mental health therapy to underserved youth, and needed a way to effectively communicate both the scope of this crisis and their organization's impact to stakeholders, donors, and the public.

Key Objectives

  • Data Visualization: Transform complex mental health statistics and impact metrics into accessible, compelling visual narratives
  • Administrative Platform: Create a secure authentication system for administrators to easily update and manage data
  • Narrative Storytelling: Develop an interactive journey showing Hopebound's role in clients' and providers' mental health experiences
  • Scalable Architecture: Build a foundation that could grow with the organization's expanding reach and data collection

Technical Implementation

The platform was built using React and Supabase.

Impact: The completed platform has significantly enhanced Hopebound's ability to communicate their mission and impact to donors, resulting in increased funding opportunities. The visualization tools have also provided valuable insights for the organization's strategic planning and resource allocation.

Skills: Project Management, Data Visualization, UX/UI Design, Team Leadership, Stakeholder Communication
